# Scribe Interview Plan

What we already know (from knowledge/, memory/, project audits):
- Who Sergey is, personality, values, work style
- All projects, their status, tech stack
- Anti-patterns, fears, blockers
- Financial goals (500K MRR), RPG metaphors
- Tools, preferences, communication style
- Edison operations (Masha, father, staff)
- MyReply product details, pricing, status

## What Scribe needs to ask (gaps in knowledge base):

### Block 1: Current Reality (April 2026)
- Where are you right now? Still in Thailand or back in Russia?
- Daily routine right now — what does a typical day look like?
- What's your energy level these days? What's different from Feb?
- Sasha — how involved is she in projects? What's her vibe on all this?

### Block 2: Edison Deep Dive
- How is Edison actually performing right now? Revenue trend?
- What does Masha need from you? What decisions are stuck?
- Father's role — what does he handle vs what he can't?
- The new menu — who decides what goes on it? What's the blocker?
- Events — are they generating money? Who books artists?
- What would it take for Edison to run 100% without you?

### Block 3: MyReply Launch Psychology
- You built everything but have 0 users. What's really stopping the launch?
- Who are 5 specific people you could send MyReply to TODAY?
- What scares you about having real users?
- If MyReply fails — what happens? What's the actual risk?
- Pricing: is 790/month right? Would you pay that?

### Block 4: Creative & Content
- "Идущий к руке" — still want to do this? What changed?
- Personal brand / blogging — what platform? What voice?
- What content do YOU consume? Who inspires you?
- If you could only post in one place, where?

### Block 5: Agency & Team Vision
- When you imagine the frogs working... what do YOU do all day?
- What decisions do you NOT want to delegate?
- What decisions do you WISH someone else would make?
- Dream scenario: phone buzzes with notification from the agency — what does it say?

### Block 6: Life Design
- 6 months from now — what does the perfect day look like?
- 1 year from now — where are you physically?
- What would make you feel like you "won"?
- What's the minimum income where you stop worrying?
- Is there something you've been avoiding thinking about?

### Interview Flow
- One question at a time
- Voice-friendly (Sergey thinks best speaking)
- After each answer: Scribe structures into knowledge_base entry
- After blocks 1-3: Scribe summarizes what he learned, asks if correct
- After all blocks: Scribe generates "Founder Profile" artifact
- Profile shared with other frogs as their foundational context
